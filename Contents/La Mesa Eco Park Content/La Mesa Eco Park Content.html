La Mesa Eco Park

Adress: La Mesa Dam Compound, Greater Lagro, Quezon City

History:

Long before it was established, La Mesa Ecopark was part of the 2,700-hectare La Mesa Watershed, which was the primary source of drinking water for Metro Manila residents, with 2,000 hectares of which are forested land. The 33-hectare public park located outside the natural boundaries of the watershed was rehabilitated in 1999. This area was named separately as La Mesa Ecopark and reopened to the public in 2004. Today, the eco-park is a destination for outdoor recreation and a true forest experience. It has blossomed into a well-preserved urban park, offering a wide range of activities and attractions for both locals and tourists, including swimming, biking, hiking, boating, fishing, and picnic areas. The park also offers educational programs and has an eco-museum and visitor center to raise awareness about environmental conservation.


Facts and Trivia:

La Mesa Eco Park is more than just a recreational area; it also plays a crucial role in environmental conservation and watershed management. It serves as an example of how urban development and environmental conservation can coexist. The park is home to diverse flora and fauna. It features numerous plant species and a variety of wildlife, making it a haven for nature lovers and birdwatchers. One of the highlights of the park is its butterfly garden. Visitors can explore this area, which is dedicated to the preservation and promotion of butterflies and their role in the ecosystem.


Additional Information:

The picturesque man-made lagoon at La Mesa Eco Park is not just for leisurely boating. It serves a dual purpose by contributing to the park's stormwater management system, which helps to reduce flooding in the surrounding areas during heavy rains. This creative design showcases the park's commitment to combining recreational amenities with eco-friendly solutions.
