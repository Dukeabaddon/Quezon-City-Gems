Mount Carmel Church

Adress: 90 4th Street, New Manila, Quezon City

History: The Minor Basilica of the National Shrine of Our Lady of Mount Carmel, commonly known as the Carmelite Church or simply the Mount Carmel Church, is a significant religious landmark in the Philippines. Located in New Manila, Quezon City, the church was established in 1954 to honor the Blessed Virgin Mary under the title of Our Lady of Mount Carmel. The history of the church is deeply intertwined with the arrival of the Carmelite friars in the Philippines in the early 20th century. The present church, built in a Gothic architectural style, was consecrated in 1958. In 1988, it was granted the status of a National Shrine by the Catholic Bishops' Conference of the Philippines, and later, in 2018, Pope Francis elevated it to the rank of a Minor Basilica. Over the years, the Minor Basilica of Our Lady of Mount Carmel has become a center of Marian devotion and continues to attract pilgrims and worshippers, which contributes to its significance as both a spiritual and cultural heritage site in the Philippines.


Facts and Trivia: The Minor Basilica is distinguished by several unique features and attributes. Apart from its religious significance, the basilica is renowned for the alleged miraculous water from a well on its grounds, drawing pilgrims seeking its reputed healing properties. The basilica's commitment to Eucharistic adoration is evident in its designated spaces for silent prayer before the Blessed Sacrament. Adjacent to the basilica is the Carmelite Monastery, where contemplative nuns lead a life of prayer, contributing to the serene spiritual atmosphere. As an international pilgrimage site, the basilica welcomes visitors from across the globe, fostering a diverse and inclusive community. With liturgical services conducted in multiple languages and a Carmelite bookstore offering religious literature, the basilica stands not only as a place of worship but as a cultural and spiritual haven that continues to evolve through ongoing renovations, ensuring its enduring significance for future generations.


Additional Information: The Minor Basilica of Our Lady of Mount Carmel houses a replica of the revered image of Our Lady of Mount Carmel from Spain. This replica is intricately enshrined within the basilica, serving as a focal point for pilgrims and devotees who seek the intercession and blessings of the Blessed Virgin Mary. The presence of this replica adds a layer of historical and cultural significance to the basilica, connecting it to the rich traditions of Mount Carmel devotion that extend beyond the Philippines.
