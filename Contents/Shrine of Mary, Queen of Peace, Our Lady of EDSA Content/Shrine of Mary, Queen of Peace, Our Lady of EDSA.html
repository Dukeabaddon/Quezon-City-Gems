Shrine of Mary, Queen of Peace, Our Lady of EDSA

Adress:EDSA, Ortigas Avenue, Quezon City

History:

The EDSA Shrine, formally known as the Shrine of Mary, Queen of Peace, Our Lady of EDSA, stands as a powerful testament to the Philippines' fight for democracy. Inaugurated on December 7, 1989, the shrine's establishment was inspired by the historic events that unfolded along Epifanio de los Santos Avenue (EDSA) during the People Power Revolution in 1986. Filipino architect Francisco Mañosa designed the shrine with a unique structure featuring a striking statue of the Virgin Mary holding a rosary, symbolizing peace and the role of faith in the country's collective struggle. The shrine gained global attention when Pope John Paul II celebrated Mass here during his visit to the Philippines in 1995. Over the years, it has undergone renovations to enhance its facilities while preserving its historical significance. Every February 25, the shrine becomes a focal point for the commemoration of the People Power Revolution, drawing pilgrims, activists, and visitors alike to reflect on the triumph of democracy over tyranny. Today, the EDSA Shrine remains a revered site, embodying the resilience and spirit of the Filipino people in their pursuit of freedom.


Facts and Trivia:

The EDSA Shrine is located at the crossroads of Epifanio de los Santos Avenue (EDSA) and Ortigas Avenue in Quezon City, Metro Manila, Philippines. The central figure of the shrine is a statue of the Virgin Mary, sculpted by Virginia Ty-Navarro. The statue holds a rosary and stands on a pillar, symbolizing peace and the role of the Virgin Mary. It is dedicated to Our Lady, who miraculously interceded to oust the dictatorship in a peaceful and bloodless uprising that is now world renowned as the People Power Revolution of 1986. Every February 25, the shrine becomes a focal point for the commemoration of the EDSA People Power Revolution. It attracts thousands of people for religious activities and reflections on democracy and freedom. In 2012, the EDSA Shrine was declared a Minor Basilica by Pope Benedict XVI, recognizing its historical and religious significance.


Additional Information:

The EDSA Shrine is not only a symbol of the Philippines' struggle for democracy but also a venue that holds the record for the largest Holy Mass attendance in history. During the visit of Pope John Paul II to the Philippines in 1995, around 5 million people gathered at the EDSA Shrine for the concluding Mass, making it one of the largest papal gatherings ever recorded. This historic event emphasized the shrine's significance not only as a symbol of political change but also as a vital spiritual and religious center for the Filipino people.
