Adress: Quezon City, Metro Manila, Philippines.

History:

The Quezon Memorial Circle is a national park located in Quezon City, Metro Manila, Philippines. The park is located inside a large traffic circle in the shape of an ellipse and bounded by the Elliptical Road and is the main park of Quezon City. The Quezon Memorial Circle was built in honor of Manuel L. Quezon, the second president of the Philippines. Quezon was a popular and respected figure, and the park was built to commemorate his life and achievements.The park was designed by Filipino architect Federico Ilustre, and construction began in 1935. The park was officially opened in 1940, but construction was interrupted by World War II. The park was finally completed in 1978.


Facts and Trivia:

The main feature of the park is the Quezon Memorial Shrine, a 66-meter (217 ft) tall mausoleum containing the remains of Manuel L. Quezon and his wife, First Lady Aurora Quezon. The shrine is located at the center of the park and is surrounded by a circular plaza. The Quezon Memorial Circle is a popular venue for events, including concerts, festivals, and political rallies. The park has also been used as a filming location for a number of movies and television shows. The park also contains a number of other attractions, including: • A Japanese garden
