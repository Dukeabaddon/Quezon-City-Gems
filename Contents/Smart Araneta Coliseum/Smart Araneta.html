Smart Araneta Coliseum

Adress: General Roxas Avenue, Cubao, Quezon

History: The Smart Araneta Coliseum, commonly known as the Big Dome, stands as an iconic and historic landmark in the heart of Quezon City, Metro Manila, Philippines. Inaugurated on March 16, 1960, it was the largest indoor arena in Asia during its early years and was commissioned by J. Amado Araneta, a prominent Filipino businessman. The coliseum quickly became a cultural and entertainment hub, hosting a diverse range of events, from sporting competitions and concerts to political rallies and religious gatherings. It gained international acclaim as the venue for the "Thrilla in Manila," the historic 1975 boxing match between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. The coliseum has undergone several renovations and expansions over the years, adapting to modern standards while maintaining its distinctive dome-shaped architecture. With a seating capacity that can accommodate tens of thousands, the Smart Araneta Coliseum remains a vital part of the Philippines' cultural and entertainment landscape, hosting major concerts, sporting events, and other significant gatherings.


Facts and Trivia: The Smart Araneta Coliseum was designed by architect Dominador Lacson Lugtu, the coliseum is known for its unique dome-shaped roof, which was considered a marvel of engineering during its time. It is an indoor multi-purpose sports arena that is part of the Araneta City in the area of Quezon City, Philippines. It is one of the largest indoor arenas in Asia, and it is also one of the largest clear span domes in the world. The Smart Araneta Coliseum is mostly used for sports such as basketball. It is a main venue of the Philippine Basketball Association and for the basketball games of the National Collegiate Athletic Association and the University Athletic Association of the Philippines. The Big Dome is also used for boxing, cockfighting, local and international concerts, circuses, religious gatherings, beauty pageants and more.


Additional Information: The Smart Araneta Coliseum played a key role in hosting the 1978 World Basketball Championship, marking the first time that the FIBA World Championship was held in Asia. This event brought together basketball teams from around the world, showcasing the coliseum as an international sports venue. Furthermore, it is not only a venue for sporting events and concerts but has also hosted major international figures and events. Notably, former U.S. President Richard Nixon delivered a speech at the coliseum during his visit to the Philippines in 1969, adding a political dimension to the coliseum's historical significance.
