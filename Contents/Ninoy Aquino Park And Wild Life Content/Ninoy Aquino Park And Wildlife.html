Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center

(NAPWC)

Adress: Diliman, Quezon City, Philippines.

History:

The Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center (NAPWC) is a 23.85-hectare (58.9-acre) urban park, located in Diliman, Quezon City, Philippines. It was named after Benigno "Ninoy" Aquino Jr., a Filipino politician who was assassinated on August 21, 1983. The park is a popular destination for families, schools, and tourists, and it offers a variety of activities and attractions, including:
• A zoo with over 1,000 animals, including mammals, birds, reptiles, and amphibians
• A botanical garden with over 1,000 plant species
• A children's playground
• A picnic area
• A lagoon for boating and fishing
• A hiking trail
• An amphitheater for concerts and other events


Facts and Trivia:

The NAPWC is also home to the National Wildlife Rescue and Research Center (NWRRC), which is a rehabilitation center for confiscated, rescued, and injured wildlife. The NWRRC provides care for over 300 animals each year, and it also works to educate the public about wildlife conservation.


Additional Information:

• The NAPWC was established in 1954.
• The park is home to over 100 species of birds.
• The park is a popular spot for birdwatching.
• The park is a member of the World Zoo and Aquarium Association (WAZA).
